Research Profile

Sustainable Digital Infrastructure under Climate and Resource Constraints

I study how AI and data-centre expansion reshape electricity demand, cooling-water requirements, and low-carbon infrastructure planning. My work aims to connect engineering-informed estimation with policy and economic analysis so that digital growth can be assessed through the lenses of decarbonization, resource efficiency, and long-term system resilience.

Profile

Research Narrative

I am currently pursuing a PhD at the University of Hong Kong, where I am refining a research agenda on the sustainable expansion of digital infrastructure. Building on earlier work in new energy systems, carbon capture, circularity, and material-flow perspectives, I now want my academic profile to foreground a tighter question: how rapid AI and data-centre growth changes infrastructure demand, resource pressure, and policy choices in cities moving toward net-zero development.

This framing keeps my prior strengths in energy systems and resource analysis, while making the doctoral story more coherent and more clearly policy-relevant.

Research Focus

Three Connected Pillars

01

Digital Infrastructure and Climate

Examine how AI and data-centre expansion creates new electricity demand and cooling-related resource pressure under urban decarbonization targets.

02

Scenario-Based Infrastructure Estimation

Compare alternative expansion pathways using assumptions on computing growth, energy efficiency, power usage effectiveness, and cooling technologies.

03

Policy and Economic Design

Evaluate which policy instruments and coordination mechanisms can reduce system pressure while supporting low-carbon digital growth.

Current Agenda

Emerging Doctoral Direction

Core Question

What level of net-zero electricity capacity could be required by AI and data-centre expansion in Hong Kong, and how do cooling choices change the resource profile of that growth?

Policy Relevance

The project can assess electricity procurement strategies, efficiency benchmarks, cooling standards, and regional coordination mechanisms rather than treating the topic as a purely technical calculation.

Research Value

This direction translates infrastructure estimation into governance trade-offs, incentive design, and implementation pathways for low-carbon digital development.
