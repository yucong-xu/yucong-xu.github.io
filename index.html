Profile

I am currently pursuing a PhD at the University of Hong Kong, supervised by Prof. Xiaoling Zhang. I study how AI and data-center expansion reshape electricity demand, cooling-water requirements, and low-carbon infrastructure planning. My work aims to connect engineering-informed estimation with policy and economic analysis so that digital growth can be assessed through the lenses of decarbonization, resource efficiency, and long-term system resilience.

Research Focus

01

Offshore Wind Expansion and Rare Earth Constraints

I study how offshore wind expansion drives demand for rare earth elements and how material supply risks may shape the pace and sustainability of energy infrastructure transitions.

02

AI Data-Centre Expansion and Electricity–Water–Resource Pressures

I examine how rapid AI and data-centre expansion reshape electricity demand, cooling-water needs, and broader resource pressures under low-carbon digital transitions.

03

Scenario-Based Policy and System Assessment

I use scenario-based approaches to evaluate alternative infrastructure expansion pathways and assess how policy design and system planning can mitigate resource bottlenecks and support sustainable development.
